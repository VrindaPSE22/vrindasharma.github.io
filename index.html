Development Economics · Environmental Economics

Water, environment, and development.

I am a PhD candidate in Economics at the Paris School of Economics working on water and environment.

About

My work combines household survey data, spatial environmental data, field experiments, and quasi-experimental methods to understand adaptation to water-related environmental risks. I am particularly interested in settings where households face both environmental constraints and institutional frictions that shape their economic choices.

My current projects study freshwater salinity in the Mekong Delta of Viet Nam, adoption of safe drinking water technologies in Gujarat, and the role of dairy cooperatives in rural structural transformation in India.

In my free time, you can find me reading about history and archaeology or at an Indian classical music concert.

Research

Freshwater Salinity and Structural Transformation in Viet Nam

Draft Available on Request

This paper studies how freshwater salinity affects agricultural productivity, household income, labor allocation, and migration in the Mekong Delta. I combine household panel data with spatial salinity measures from a hydrological model to examine how a sector-specific climate shock shapes local adaptation and structural transformation.

Joint work with Sepehr Eslami.

Abstract

Barriers to Safe Water Technology Adoption in Rural India

Pilot in progress

This project evaluates interventions to increase adoption of community reverse osmosis plants in Gujarat. The experiment studies whether information, habit formation, and reductions in unequal collection costs can increase take-up of safe drinking water technologies at low prices.

Joint work with Balasai Vanukuri.

Dairy Cooperatives and Rural Transformation in India

Work in progress

This project studies how cooperative institutions and dairy market access affected rural livelihoods, infrastructure, and long-term structural transformation.

Joint work with Balasai Vanukuri.

Menstrual Stigma, Hygiene, and Human Capital: Experimental Evidence from India

Pilot in progress

This project studies whether interventions targeting menstrual stigma, menstrual health knowledge, and school environments can improve girls’ wellbeing and educational outcomes in India. The study examines how social norms and psychosocial barriers shape human capital accumulation during adolescence.

Joint work with Karen Macours and Duncan Webb.
